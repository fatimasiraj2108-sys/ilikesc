Check your eligibility

Before you apply, it helps to understand how eligibility is decided. This section explains the criteria in full, lets you run a quick estimate, and answers the questions we get asked most.

Each scholarship track has its own income and academic thresholds, because the cost of tuition and the competitiveness of applicants differ by level and city. The table below shows exactly what each track looks for. These are guidelines, not hard cut-offs — every full application is still read by a reviewer, and reviewers can and do make exceptions for difficult circumstances explained in your essay.

What counts as "household income"?

We mean the combined monthly income of everyone currently contributing to your household — usually your parents or guardians, and any older siblings who are working. If income is irregular (e.g. daily-wage work), use a fair monthly average. You'll be asked to share a Google Drive link to proof of this (a salary slip, an employer letter, or a income certificate from your union council).

What if I don't clearly qualify?

Apply anyway. The numbers above are starting guidelines, not a strict cut-off. Reviewers weigh your full circumstances — number of dependents, medical or family hardship, and your academic trajectory — not just two numbers. Use the essay to explain anything the form doesn't capture.

Frequently asked questions

Can I apply to more than one scholarship track?

Apply to the one track that matches your current grade level and city. If you're unsure which one fits, use the eligibility checker above or ask the chatbot.

What documents will I need?

Your latest mark sheet, proof of household income, and a copy of your CNIC or B-form. You won't upload files directly — you'll share Google Drive links instead (explained in the application form below).

Is there a fee to apply?

No. Checking eligibility and applying are completely free. If anyone asks you to pay, it isn't part of this programme — please report it.

How long does review take?

Typically two to three weeks after your track's deadline. You'll be contacted using the phone number on your application, so make sure it's correct and reachable.

What happens if my application is approved?

A programme coordinator will contact your parent or guardian directly to confirm your details and walk through the next steps, including how funds are disbursed to your school.

Can my parent or guardian apply on my behalf?

Yes — a guardian can fill out the form for you. Just make sure the student's own details (name, marks, CNIC) are entered accurately, since that's who the scholarship is for.
