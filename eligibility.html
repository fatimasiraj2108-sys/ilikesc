Check your eligibility

Before you apply, it helps to understand how eligibility is decided. This page explains the criteria in full and gives you an estimated percentage chance of being accepted onto the track you pick — based on your marks and household income compared to that track's usual range.

Each scholarship track has its own income and academic thresholds, because the cost of tuition and the competitiveness of applicants differ by level and city. The table below shows exactly what each track looks for. These are guidelines, not hard cut-offs — every full application is still read by a reviewer, and reviewers can and do make exceptions for difficult circumstances explained in your essay.

What counts as "household income"?

We mean the combined monthly income of everyone currently contributing to your household — usually your parents or guardians, and any older siblings who are working. If income is irregular (e.g. daily-wage work), use a fair monthly average.

What if my percentage is low?

The number below is a rough estimate, not a decision. Reviewers weigh your full circumstances — dependents, medical or family hardship, and your academic trajectory — not just two numbers. Apply anyway and use the essay to explain anything the form doesn't capture.
